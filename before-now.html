Before I Think,
Now I Know

A reflection routine that helps students recognize how their thinking has changed and grown over time. Perfect for building metacognitive awareness and celebrating learning progress.

How Before-Now Works

Before I Think

What did I used to believe or think about this topic?

"I used to think..."
→

Now I Know

How has my thinking changed or evolved?

"Now I think..."

The Power of Reflection

Why Use This Routine?

  • • Builds metacognitive awareness
  • • Celebrates learning growth
  • • Makes learning visible to students
  • • Encourages reflective thinking
  • • Helps students value their progress

Best Times to Use

  • • End of a unit or topic
  • • After a significant learning experience
  • • During portfolio reviews
  • • Before and after field trips
  • • When studying complex concepts

Student Reflections

Learning About Seasons

  • Student reflection on seasonal changes

  • Documentation of thinking evolution

Learning About Seasons

Before I Think:

"I used to think winter was just cold and I didn't like it because we couldn't play outside."

- Maria, age 5 (September)

Now I Know:

"Now I think winter is important because the trees need to rest, and I love seeing snowflakes. We can build snowmen and drink hot chocolate!"

- Maria, age 5 (January)

Tips for Success

Getting Started

  • • Start with simple, concrete topics
  • • Model your own thinking changes
  • • Create a safe space for reflection
  • • Celebrate all thinking growth
  • • Use visual documentation

Advanced Practice

  • • Connect to learning standards
  • • Use with individual conferences
  • • Create class reflection books
  • • Involve families in reflection
  • • Document long-term growth
