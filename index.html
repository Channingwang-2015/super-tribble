About Harvard Project Zero

Project Zero is an educational research group at the Harvard Graduate School of Education. Their mission is to understand and enhance learning, thinking, and creativity in all disciplines.

Thinking Routines are simple structures that help students observe, organize, reason, and reflect on ideas. They make thinking visible and create a culture of curiosity in the classroom.

In kindergarten, these routines help young learners develop metacognitive awareness - the ability to think about their own thinking.

Key Benefits

  • • Encourages critical thinking
  • • Develops communication
  • • Fosters curiosity
  • • Makes learning visible

Explore Thinking Routines

See-Think-Wonder

A powerful routine that encourages careful observation, thoughtful interpretation, and curiosity. Perfect for exploring images, objects, and ideas.

Best for: Introducing new topics, exploring art, examining objects

Before I Think
Now I Know

A reflection routine that helps students recognize how their thinking has changed and grown over time. Builds metacognitive awareness.

Best for: End of units, reflection, showing growth

Color-Symbol-Image

A creative routine that helps students express understanding through visual metaphors. Encourages abstract thinking and personal connections.

Best for: Synthesizing ideas, creative expression, character analysis
